# About me — Maya Okafor

## One-page read
Maya Okafor is a 24-year-old woman living in a small supported living service in Sheffield, South Yorkshire. Diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Condition (Level 1), Generalised Anxiety Disorder, and Selective Mutism, Maya communicates with staff primarily through non-verbal means, using a notes app on her phone and a whiteboard in her room. She is fluent with her family but selectively mute in most interactions at Linden House. Her interests include botanical drawing and listening to audiobooks, and she finds comfort in the familiar environment of her home with her mum and cat, Pepper.

Maya has a close-knit family, with her mum, Grace, being her primary contact. Her dad, Stephen, visits monthly, while her sister Ada connects with her weekly via FaceTime. Maya is sensitive to loud noises and unexpected changes in her routine, which can trigger anxiety. However, she has found solace in the small garden at Linden House, where she enjoys sketching nature. Recently, Maya has shown signs of trust with a new support worker, Joel, indicating potential for building stronger relationships within her support network.

## My life so far
Maya was born and raised in Sheffield, South Yorkshire. She grew up in a supportive family environment with her mum, Grace, who works in a pharmacy, her dad, Stephen, and her older sister, Ada. Her parents separated when she was a teenager, but they maintained a positive co-parenting relationship, ensuring that Maya and Ada felt supported.

Maya attended a local comprehensive school, where she excelled academically but struggled with social interactions due to her autism and anxiety. After completing her A-levels, she opted not to pursue higher education immediately, instead focusing on her interests in art and literature from the comfort of her family home.

In 2024, Maya moved into Linden House, a supported living service, to gain more independence while still receiving the support she needed. The transition was challenging, but she gradually adapted to her new environment with the help of her keyworker and the dedicated staff team. Over time, she established a routine that included drawing, listening to audiobooks, and spending time in the garden.
