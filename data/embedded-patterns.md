# Embedded patterns: the demo's answer key

> **Purpose.** These are the five things the refreshed care plan should surface that the **outdated plan does not contain**. They are visible across the notes corpus. They are the demo's grading rubric: if the refresh finds all five with traceable evidence, the demo is real. If it misses any, the demo is theatre — and you adjust the prompt / pipeline until it does.

Each pattern is structured the same way:

- **Pattern.** The thing the refresh should detect.
- **Window.** When in the notes it becomes visible.
- **Evidence trail.** Approximate count and type of notes that contain signal.
- **Refreshed-plan target.** Where in the refreshed plan the pattern should land, and roughly how it should be worded.
- **Verification.** Binary pass/fail the tester (you, then Ellie) can check.

---

## Pattern 1 — New sensory preference: white noise at bedtime

**Pattern.** Sam has begun consistently asking for white noise (specifically a rain-sound app on his iPad) at bedtime. He sleeps significantly better with it on. This started around February 2026 and is now reliable.

**Window.** First mention: late January 2026 (seed notes). Establishes as a pattern from mid-February. Reliable signal by April 2026.

**Evidence trail.** Expect ~15-25 evening / night-shift notes referencing the iPad rain app, white noise, "rain" Makaton sign, or improved sleep when it's used. A handful of nights without it show poorer sleep — the contrast IS the evidence.

**Refreshed-plan target.**
- Section: **Sensory preferences** (currently absent from the outdated plan).
- Suggested wording: *"Sam has shown a clear preference for white-noise (specifically rain sounds via his iPad app) at bedtime. Nights with white noise show consistent settling within 20-30 minutes; nights without show settling times of 60+ minutes and increased pacing. Staff should ensure the iPad is charged and the app is queued before Sam goes to bed."*
- The refreshed goal section should add: *"Maintain consistent white-noise bedtime routine. Review every 6 weeks for ongoing preference."*

**Verification.** The refreshed plan must (a) name "white noise / rain sounds" specifically, (b) connect it to bedtime, (c) cite at least 3 source notes with dates. If any of those three is missing, fail.

## Pattern 2 — Changing seizure pattern: clustering in afternoons

**Pattern.** Sam's breakthrough seizures have shifted from being evenly distributed across the day to clustering in the early afternoon (between approximately 13:30 and 15:30). Over the last 60 days, 6 of 7 breakthrough seizures have fallen in this window. This is clinically significant and should trigger a GP / neurology conversation.

**Window.** Pattern emerges from March 2026 onwards. Visible as a clear cluster only when seizure log notes are looked at together.

**Evidence trail.** Expect ~6-8 seizure log notes across March-May 2026, with timestamps. The cluster is in the timestamps, not in the prose.

**Refreshed-plan target.**
- Section: **Health → Epilepsy / Seizure management**.
- Suggested wording: *"Over the 60 days prior to this plan refresh, Sam has experienced 6 breakthrough focal seizures, 5 of which occurred between 13:30 and 15:30. This represents a clear afternoon clustering pattern not present in the previous 12 months. RECOMMENDED ACTION: schedule GP review and request neurology referral to consider medication timing review (current Lamotrigine and Levetiracetam are dosed 07:30 / 19:30 — afternoon trough may be a factor)."*
- The refreshed plan must flag this as **a recommended action**, not just an observation. This is the clinical-value signal that justifies the £200.

**Verification.** The refreshed plan must (a) report seizure count and timestamp clustering, (b) name the afternoon window, (c) make a specific clinical recommendation (medication review). Cites at least 4 seizure log entries.

## Pattern 3 — Reduced tolerance for supermarket visits

**Pattern.** Sam's tolerance for the weekly supermarket trip has declined over the period. In January he engaged well. By April, supermarket visits result in pacing, vocalisation, and (twice) requests to leave before completing the shop. This is a safeguarding signal — not yet a crisis, but a clear shift.

**Window.** Baseline January 2026 (positive supermarket notes in seeds). Decline emerges from February. Clear pattern by April.

**Evidence trail.** Expect ~12-15 supermarket-related notes across the period. Early ones positive, late ones increasingly negative. Two incident reports in April reference Sam asking to leave the shop.

**Refreshed-plan target.**
- Section: **PBS → Triggers and antecedents**.
- Suggested wording: *"Sam's tolerance for routine supermarket visits has reduced markedly over the last 90 days. Where January notes describe Sam engaging well, April notes describe pacing, vocalisation, and requests to leave. Two incidents (08/04 and 19/04) involved leaving the shop before completion. RECOMMENDED ACTION: reduce supermarket exposure to fortnightly, switch to a quieter local store rather than the large supermarket, and use the visual day plan to flag the visit in advance."*
- The refreshed risk assessment should add a "sensory overload in retail environments" risk with a low/medium severity flag.

**Verification.** The refreshed plan must (a) name the declining trend (not just one bad day), (b) cite at least one positive January note AND at least one negative April note for contrast, (c) recommend a specific environmental adjustment.

## Pattern 4 — New positive friendship with Mia at day service

**Pattern.** Sam has formed a friendship with a peer at day service called Mia. They sit together at lunch, share interest in trains, and Sam has begun asking for "Mia day?" at home before day-service days. This is a positive outcome signal.

**Window.** First mention January 2026 (a single mention in seed notes). Builds across February. Established by March. Sam mentions Mia at home from late February.

**Evidence trail.** Expect ~20+ notes referencing Mia: day service activity notes, lunch notes, Sam asking about Mia at home, brief incident where Mia was absent and Sam was unsettled.

**Refreshed-plan target.**
- Section: **Social and emotional wellbeing** / **Family, friends and network**.
- Suggested wording: *"Sam has developed a friendship with Mia, a peer at his day service. They share an interest in trains and consistently sit together at lunch. Sam now spontaneously asks about Mia at home before day-service mornings, which is a significant social-communication development. RECOMMENDED ACTION: explore with the day service whether Sam and Mia might be supported to attend train-watching outings together; document the friendship in Sam's social network table."*
- The refreshed plan's network table must add Mia as a named friend (this is currently a `[FILL IN]` slot in the outdated plan).

**Verification.** The refreshed plan must (a) name Mia, (b) describe the friendship specifically (not generically "Sam has peer relationships"), (c) cite at least 3 source notes, (d) propose a concrete supported activity.

## Pattern 5 — New communication skill: Makaton sign "more"

**Pattern.** Sam has begun using the Makaton sign for "more" reliably and spontaneously. He uses it in three specific contexts: requesting more food at meals, asking for the train-station visit to extend, and asking for more time on his iPad. This is a meaningful expansion of his expressive vocabulary.

**Window.** First emerges March 2026. Used in multiple contexts by April. Established skill by May.

**Evidence trail.** Expect ~10-15 notes referencing Sam signing "more" or staff observing the sign. Should appear in different contexts (food, transport, leisure) to show generalisation.

**Refreshed-plan target.**
- Section: **Communication → Communication passport** AND **Goals**.
- Suggested wording in passport: *"Sam now uses the Makaton sign for 'more' reliably and spontaneously in at least three contexts: requesting more food at mealtimes, requesting extension of preferred activities (train-watching, iPad time), and at the end of family video calls. Staff should respond to 'more' as an active communicative initiation, not a behavioural request — confirm what 'more' refers to and respond accordingly."*
- A new SMART goal: *"By end of August 2026, Sam will be able to use the 'more' sign in five distinct contexts, supported by staff modelling and visual reinforcement. Reviewed monthly via Makaton tracking sheet."*

**Verification.** The refreshed plan must (a) name "more" specifically as a new sign, (b) name at least two distinct contexts where Sam uses it, (c) cite at least 3 source notes, (d) propose a measurable communication goal that wasn't in the outdated plan.

## How to use this file when grading the demo

When you run the refresh pipeline against the dataset, the output should be:

1. A refreshed plan markdown file.
2. An `evidence-trail.json` mapping every claim in the refreshed plan to a list of source note IDs and dates.

You grade the demo against this file with a simple checklist:

| Pattern | Named? | Specific? | Source notes cited? | Action recommended? | PASS / FAIL |
|---------|--------|-----------|---------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| 1. White noise | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N (≥3) | Y/N | |
| 2. Afternoon seizures | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N (≥4) | Y/N | |
| 3. Supermarket tolerance | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N (≥2, contrast) | Y/N | |
| 4. Mia friendship | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N (≥3) | Y/N | |
| 5. "More" sign | Y/N | Y/N | Y/N (≥3) | Y/N | |

5/5 PASS = demo is ready to show Ellie.
4/5 PASS = demo is ready to show Ellie with one known gap to discuss.
≤3/5 PASS = adjust the prompt or pipeline. Do not show.

Score your first run honestly. The second pass is usually the one that gets to 5/5.
